Playbook · v1 · 2026

Turning color talk into the right samples.

One repeatable way to go from a client's first mention of "warm walnut" to a confirmed moodboard and a tight set of physical samples — so we stop the re-sends, the swatch-vs-reality surprises, and the "is this 7707 or Essence" mix-ups.

The one rule

Moodboard first, samples second. We never order a physical sample until the client has confirmed a color direction on a moodboard built from real kitchen photos.

02

The process — four steps

1

Collect the color data

Three inputs, and you go after all three on purpose — don't wait for the client to volunteer them.

  • On the call: ask directly — wood color, solid color, and what they dislike ("not ashy", "not stark white", "not too yellow"). Dislikes are the most useful part.
  • References: proactively ask for kitchens they love — Pinterest, photos, links.
  • Catalog: proactively walk them through our inspiration catalog so they react to real options, not abstractions.
Translate as you go. "Walnut" almost always means our stained-oak-that-reads-as-walnut; "white" splits into warm white / off-white / beige. Capture the intent, not just the word.
2

Build the moodboard

One deck. Minimum 2 slides per candidate color, minimum 3 inspiration photos per slide.

  • Inspiration photo = a full real kitchen photo. Never a swatch or a sample photo.
  • Use our completed projects first, then catalog / web photos if needed.
  • Label every slide with the catalog color name — not an internal code.
  • Pair wood with the neutral they lean to — "Golden oak + off-white", "Walnut + greige" — that's how people picture a kitchen.
3

Confirm with the customer

  • Confirm — "yes, that's us." Direction locked.
  • Feedback — refine and re-share until it's confirmed (a quick call usually does it).
Set expectations here. Small samples read more muted and colder than a real kitchen. Say it before they open the box, and lean on the moodboard photos + a short video for the true read. Natural oak with 5% lacquer reads warmer / more orange than a raw swatch.
4

Hand off — Slack + Airtable

Only once the moodboard is confirmed:

  • Post the confirmed moodboard in the client's dedicated Slack project channel, tagging the team + Valentyna.
  • Create the Airtable sample task with the selected samples, chosen from the sample catalog and filtered by color.
  • Valentyna curates → photographs before shipping → ships (Chicago stock = fast; Ukraine ≈ up to 14 days) → posts tracking.
  • Bathroom or vanity too? Collect those colors so everything ships together.
03

Standards

Do

  • Confirm the moodboard before ordering anything
  • Use real full-kitchen photos as inspiration
  • Keep it to about 5 samples
  • Use catalog names; confirm the code ↔ name in the database
  • Ask tangential vs radial cut for wood samples
  • Photograph samples before they ship

Don't

  • Order samples off a hunch or before confirmation
  • Use a swatch photo as "inspiration"
  • Dump 9+ samples and overwhelm them
  • Ship on a raw code alone (e.g. "7707")
  • Assume "walnut" means walnut wood — it's usually stained oak
  • Promise a match on a painted RAL tone without flagging the price tier

05

FAQ — what if → what we do

What if the client only talks vaguely about color on the call?
Pin it down with dislikes and our catalog. Show 3–4 real kitchens and ask "more like this, or this?" Capture wood pref + solid pref + one or two hard nos. That's enough to start a moodboard.
What if we don't have a real kitchen photo for a color?
Use a completed project in the same family first (mid-tone and golden live together, etc.). If we genuinely have none, use a high-quality web photo of a real kitchen — never a swatch. Note in the slide it's a reference, not our build.
What if the client says they want "walnut"?
Show both: real American walnut and stained-oak-that-reads-as-walnut. We usually deliver the walnut look as stained oak — cheaper, cleaner grain. Confirm whether they care about the color or the actual species.
What if the sample looks muted / cold / "whitewashed" to them?
Expected — small swatches always read flatter than a real kitchen under real light. Point them back to the moodboard photos + a short video. Offer the natural + 5% lacquer version, which reads warmer. If needed, Valentyna reshoots under good lighting.
What if the client wants "white"?
Split it: warm white vs off-white vs beige-y neutral. Off-white = RAL 9010, which is painted and a higher price tier — flag that early. Show the two or three closest catalog options side by side.
What if the code and the name don't match in the database?
The name wins. Don't ship on a code alone. Ping Valentyna to confirm the entry before the task goes out.
What if the client wants more than 5 samples?
Default is about 5 so we don't overwhelm them. Trim to the strongest options that match the moodboard. The exception is testing a new supplier line (e.g. Adler) — a couple extra is fine there.
What if the moodboard isn't confirmed but the client is pushing for samples?
Still confirm direction first — even a five-minute "yes, that's the vibe." Ordering before that is exactly what creates re-sends. You can ship the same week once it's confirmed.
What if nobody specified tangential vs radial cut?
Ask before ordering — it changes the grain look a lot. Default for the warm / organic look is tangential ("flat" or "cross-section" cut).
What if the client has a bathroom or vanity too?
Collect those colors in the same round and ship all samples together in one box. Saves a shipment and a wait.
What if the client is in a hurry?
Ship whatever we hold in Chicago stock immediately; the rest follows from Ukraine (~up to 14 days). Set that expectation up front and post tracking in the channel.
What if they ask what samples cost?
Samples are refundable minus about $30 each if they decide not to proceed. Frame it as risk-free — they get the real materials in their own home, under their own light.
What if the client changes their mind after confirming?
Update the moodboard, re-confirm the new direction, then adjust the Airtable task before Valentyna ships. The moodboard always stays the single source of truth.
What if there's no Slack channel for the project yet?
Create it, add the team + Valentyna, then post the confirmed moodboard there. Every project handoff lives in its own channel.
